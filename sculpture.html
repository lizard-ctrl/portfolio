"Don't Let The Bugs Bite" 2019

This sculpture uses physical computing to create sound and movement when a viewer stands or lays directly under the canopy. It plays on the very common fear of bugs and trys to create an environment where one can question this.



"They Can Talk Too!" 2019

One of the first intimate connections a child makes outside of their mother is with a transitional object, typically a stuffed animal or blanket. In this sculpture I explore that connection by creating movement in the abandoned stuffed animals as the viewer gets close to it by implementing physical computing.
